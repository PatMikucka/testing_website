🌿 Ingredients & Making

🕯️ Burning & Care

My 6oz amber jar candles have a burn time of 35–40 hours. My 8oz ceramic pot candles burn for 45–50 hours. These estimates are based on ideal conditions: trimmed wick, draft-free room, burns of 2–4 hours at a time.
Tunnelling almost always happens because the first burn was cut short. On your very first light, you need to burn the candle until the entire surface has melted from edge to edge — this sets the "memory" of the wax. For a 6oz jar, that's usually 2–3 hours. If tunnelling has already started, there's a technique using foil to create a "dome" that can help correct it — get in touch and I'll walk you through it.
Before every burn, trim your wick to approximately 5mm (¼ inch). A wick trimmer is ideal, but nail scissors work well. Always trim when the wax is cool and solid. Remove any wick debris from the wax before relighting.
A small amount of soot is normal, but if you're seeing heavy black marks on the inside of the jar, the wick is almost certainly too long. Extinguish the candle, let it cool, trim the wick to 5mm, and try again. Draughts can also cause excessive sooting — make sure the candle isn't near a fan or open window.
