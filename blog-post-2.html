How to Get the Most Out of Your Candle

By Lila · February 24, 2026

A good candle deserves to be burned well. I've put a lot of thought into every aspect of these candles — the wax blend, the wick size, the essential oil ratios — but none of that matters if the first burn goes wrong.

Here's everything I know about getting a long, beautiful, safe burn from your candle.

The First Burn Is Everything

Wax has memory. This isn't metaphorical — it literally sets the precedent for every future burn. On your very first light, you should allow the candle to burn until the entire top layer of wax has melted from edge to edge. This creates a full melt pool.

If you blow it out too early, the wax will "tunnel" — digging a hole down the middle while the outer wax remains solid, never to melt again. You'll lose potentially 30–40% of your candle.

🕯️ First burn tip

For a 6oz jar, allow 2–3 hours on the first burn. For an 8oz pot, you may need 3–4 hours. Don't rush it.

Trim Your Wick Every Single Time

Before every burn, trim your wick to about 5mm (¼ inch). A wick that's too long produces a large, sooty flame that burns through the wax too quickly and may leave black marks inside the jar.

You can buy a proper wick trimmer (they're excellent), but nail scissors work just as well. Just make sure the candle is cool and all debris is removed before lighting.

"A trimmed wick is the single biggest thing you can do for a longer, cleaner, more fragrant burn."

How Long Should You Burn It?

Aim for burns of 2–4 hours maximum. Burning for too long causes the wick to "mushroom" (develop a carbon ball on top), raises the temperature of the jar, and can compromise the structural integrity of the container over time.

If you notice the flame getting very large or flickering heavily, that's usually a sign you've been burning for too long or the wick needs trimming.

Where to Put Your Candle

  • Away from draughts (fans, open windows, air vents) — these cause uneven burning and excessive soot
  • On a heat-resistant surface, away from flammable materials
  • Out of direct sunlight, which can cause the wax to discolour
  • Away from children and pets
  • Not in an enclosed space with no ventilation

Storing Your Candle Between Burns

Natural wax is sensitive to temperature and UV light. Store your candle with the lid on (if it came with one) in a cool, dark place. Avoid storing in a warm kitchen or a sunny windowsill.

If dust collects on the wax surface, gently wipe it off with a soft cloth before burning — dust on the wax can cause uneven burning.

When to Stop Burning

Stop burning your candle when there's about 1cm (½ inch) of wax remaining. Burning past this point can cause the base of the jar to overheat.

And then — don't throw it away. Those amber glass jars are made to last. Clean them out with boiling water, peel off the label, and you have a beautiful little vessel for anything from cotton buds to fresh herbs.

🌿 To clean your jar

Pour boiling water into the jar and allow it to sit until the remaining wax floats to the surface and solidifies. Scoop it out, drain the water, and wash as normal.
